Our Commitment to Accessibility

Elegant Construction Inc. is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone, and applying the relevant accessibility standards to ensure we provide equal access to all of our users.

We believe that the web should be accessible to all, and we strive to ensure that our website is usable by people of all abilities and disabilities. As a California-based company, we take our obligations under federal and state accessibility laws seriously, including the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act, and California's Unruh Civil Rights Act (Cal. Civ. Code §51 et seq.).

Conformance Standards

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA as published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines define how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide array of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, language, learning, and neurological disabilities.

WCAG 2.1 Level AA is built on four principles of accessibility:

  • Perceivable: Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive
  • Operable: User interface components and navigation must be operable by all users
  • Understandable: Information and the operation of user interface must be understandable
  • Robust: Content must be robust enough to be interpreted by a wide variety of user agents, including assistive technologies

Accessibility Measures

Elegant Construction Inc. takes the following measures to ensure accessibility of our website:

  • Include accessibility as part of our mission statement and company values
  • Integrate accessibility into our web development and procurement processes
  • Conduct regular accessibility audits using both automated tools and manual testing
  • Provide accessibility training for our web content authors and developers
  • Assign clear accessibility goals and responsibilities within our organization
  • Employ formal accessibility quality assurance methods before publishing new content

Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy (H1–H6)
Descriptive alt text for all meaningful images and graphics
ARIA labels and landmarks for assistive technology navigation
Sufficient color contrast ratios meeting WCAG AA standards (4.5:1 minimum)
Full keyboard navigation support for all interactive elements
Visible focus indicators for keyboard users
Responsive design that works across all devices and screen sizes
Form labels and error messages clearly associated with inputs
Skip navigation links for screen reader users
Resizable text up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
No content that flashes more than three times per second
Consistent navigation and page structure across the site

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some areas of the website may not yet be fully accessible. We are aware of the following limitations and are actively working to resolve them:

  • Third-party content: Some third-party content, such as embedded maps or social media feeds, may not fully meet accessibility standards. We do not have direct control over the accessibility of third-party content but make every effort to choose accessible solutions.
  • PDF documents: Some older PDF documents on our site may not be fully accessible. We are working to remediate these documents and provide accessible alternatives upon request.
  • Video content: We are in the process of adding closed captions to all video content. Alternative text descriptions are available upon request.

We are committed to continuously monitoring and improving the accessibility of our website. If you encounter an accessibility barrier, please contact us so we can address it promptly.

Compatible Assistive Technologies

Our website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack)
  • Screen magnification software (ZoomText, Windows Magnifier)
  • Speech recognition software (Dragon NaturallySpeaking)
  • Keyboard-only navigation
  • Switch access devices
  • High-contrast and custom color modes

Our website is best experienced in the latest versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge browsers.

Applicable Laws & Enforcement

This accessibility statement is provided in compliance with the following laws and regulations:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Title III: Prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability in places of public accommodation, including websites operated by businesses open to the public.
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act: Requires federal agencies and their contractors to make electronic and information technology accessible to people with disabilities.
  • California's Unruh Civil Rights Act (Cal. Civ. Code §51): Provides broad protection against discrimination by business establishments in California, including through digital platforms. California courts have recognized ADA violations as per se violations of the Unruh Act.
  • California Government Code §11135: Requires state-funded programs and activities to be accessible to persons with disabilities.
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1: Internationally recognized technical standards for web accessibility published by the W3C.

California SB 459 – Website Accessibility

  • California Senate Bill 459 (effective January 1, 2024) added provisions to the Unruh Civil Rights Act specifically addressing website accessibility. Businesses are encouraged to comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards to provide equal access to digital services.
  • For more information on California accessibility requirements, visit the California Department of Rehabilitation.
