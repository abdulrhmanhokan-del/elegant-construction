What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide a better browsing experience, and give site owners reporting information. Cookies allow the website to recognize your device and remember certain information about your visit, such as your preferences and settings.

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies and similar tracking technologies we use on our website at www.elegantcon.com (the "Site"), why we use them, and how you can control them. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which provides more detail on how we process personal information.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential site functionality: To ensure the site works properly, including navigation, form submissions, and security features
  • Performance and analytics: To understand how visitors interact with our site, which pages are most popular, and to identify areas for improvement
  • User preferences: To remember your preferences and settings so you don't have to re-enter them each time you visit
  • Security: To help protect our site and users from security threats

Types of Cookies

Cookies can be categorized by their lifespan and by who sets them:

By Lifespan

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are stored in your browser's memory only during your browsing session. They are automatically deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period of time or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences across multiple visits.

By Source

  • First-Party Cookies: Set by Elegant Construction Inc. (the website you are visiting) directly.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by services other than our website, such as analytics providers. These are described in detail below.

In addition to cookies, we may use the following tracking technologies:

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie rights in the following ways:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or alert you when a cookie is being set. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data

Important Notice

  • If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our Site may not function properly. You will still be able to browse our Site, but certain interactive features (such as form submissions and preferences) may be limited.
  • Disabling cookies in your browser settings applies to all websites, not just ours. Be aware of the broader impact before making changes.

Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can prevent Google Analytics from collecting data about your visits to our Site by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. This add-on instructs the Google Analytics JavaScript not to send visit information to Google Analytics.

Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers feature a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting that sends a signal to websites you visit, indicating that you do not wish to be tracked. There is currently no universally accepted standard for how companies should respond to DNT signals.

In accordance with the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA), we disclose our practice regarding DNT signals: at this time, our Site does not respond to DNT signals in browsers. However, we limit our data collection and tracking practices as described in this Cookie Policy and our Privacy Policy.

California law requires us to disclose how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there is no industry or legal standard for recognizing or honoring DNT signals, we do not currently respond to them. We will revisit this policy if a standard is established.
